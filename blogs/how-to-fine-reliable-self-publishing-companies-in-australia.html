Self Publishing Companies Australia: How to choose the right one


Author: James Wilson

Published April 30, 2026

Finding the right self publishing companies Australia is not as simple as comparing prices. Many services promise quick results, but few deliver consistent quality across editing, design, and distribution. Authors often realise this too late.

At Self Publishing Australia, we have supported 500+ authors with the expertise of 90+ professionals. The difference always comes down to one thing. Clarity. When authors understand what to look for, they avoid poor decisions and publish stronger books.

This guide explains how to identify reliable companies and what to question before committing.


The problem: Too many choices, too little quality


Australia has dozens of self publishing services. Some are excellent. Some are average. Some are dangerous.

The dangerous ones look professional. They have nice websites. They use fancy language. They promise bestsellers. Then they deliver nothing.

How do you tell the difference? You need a system. You need to know what questions to ask. You need to know which red flags to watch for.

Let us build your vetting checklist.


Red flag one: They promise bestseller status


No company can guarantee your book will be a bestseller. That is a lie. Bestseller status depends on your book quality, your marketing, and luck.

If a company says, “We will make your book number one on Amazon”, walk away. They cannot control thousands of readers.

Honest companies say “We will produce a professional book. Sales depend on your effort and audience.”

Self Publishing Australia never promises what we cannot deliver. We guarantee quality work. We do not guarantee sales figures.


Red flag two: They charge thousands for basic services


Some so-called self publishing companies Australia charge $10,000 for editing, a cover, and layout. That is three times the fair price.

Professional editing for a standard novel costs $1,500 to $3,000. A good custom cover costs $800 to $1,500. Layout costs $400 to $800. Any company charging double is taking advantage.

How to check: Get three quotes. Compare line by line. If one quote is dramatically higher, ask why. If they cannot explain, move on.


Red flag three: They keep your rights


Read the contract. Does it say you transfer copyright to the company? Never sign that.

You must keep all rights to your book. A service provider helps you publish. They do not own your words. They do not own your cover art. They do not own your ISBN.

Legitimate self publishing companies Australia work for you. You hire them. You pay them. You own everything.

If a contract asks for rights or royalties, run. That is not self publishing. That is a vanity press or a scam.


Red flag four: No clear pricing on their website


Honest companies list prices. They show packages. They explain what is included.

Dishonest companies hide pricing. They want you to call. Then they pressure you. Then they invent a custom price that changes based on what they think you will pay.

What to do: Do not engage with any company that refuses to list prices. Transparency is the minimum standard.

Self Publishing Australia shows every price on our home page and pricing page. No hidden numbers. No calls required.


The five questions you must ask every company


Before signing anything, ask these questions.

  • Question one: Who does the work? Do you have in-house staff or freelancers? Ask for names and portfolios.
  • Question two: What is included in the price? Revision rounds? File formats? Print ready PDFs? Get it in writing.
  • Question three: What happens if I am unhappy? Do you offer revisions? Do you have a dispute process?
  • Question four: Do I keep all rights? Get a clear yes. No shared royalties. No copyright transfer.
  • Question five: Can I speak to past clients? A confident company will say yes. A scared company will say no.

Self Publishing Australia answers these questions instantly. We have over 90 experts on our team. We connect you with past clients on request.


Where to find reliable self publishing companies in Australia


Start with these sources.

  • Professional organisations: Look for members of the Independent Book Publishers Association (IBPA) or similar. Membership requires ethical standards.
  • Author communities: Ask in Australian writing groups on Facebook or Reddit. Other authors share honest experiences.
  • Reviews on multiple platforms: Google, Trustpilot, and the Better Business Bureau. Read the bad reviews. See how the company responds.
  • Referrals from editors or designers: Freelancers often know which companies are good. They see the work come across their desks.

Avoid companies that only advertise on social media with no track record. Avoid companies that pressure you to sign today.


What a reliable company looks like


Let me describe a trustworthy self publishing companies Australia partner.

  • They list prices clearly on their website.
  • They explain what each service includes.
  • They offer sample edits or cover concepts before payment.
  • They have published authors you can contact.
  • They respond to emails within 24 hours.
  • They do not call you repeatedly after a quote.
  • They put everything in writing.
  • They let you keep all rights.

Self Publishing Australia matches every point on this list. We have helped over 500 authors. We have worked with 90+ publishing experts. We are transparent, responsive, and fair.

Ready to work with a reliable partner? Get a free quote from our team. No pressure. No hidden fees.


The difference between self publishing companies and vanity presses


Many authors confuse these. Let me clarify.

  • Self publishing company: You pay for services. You keep all rights and royalties. You control the process. You are the publisher.
  • Vanity press: You pay a large fee. They claim to publish your book. But they keep rights. They take royalties. They control pricing. You have no say.

Vanity presses often call themselves self publishing. They are not.

How to tell: Ask “Who owns the ISBN?” If they say, “We do”, that is a vanity press. On legitimate self publishing, you own the ISBN. You are the publisher of record.


What to do if you have been scammed


Unfortunately, some authors come to us after losing money. Here is what to do.

  • Stop all communication with the company.
  • Contact your bank or credit card provider. Dispute the charge.
  • Report the company to the ACCC (Australian Competition and Consumer Commission).
  • Leave honest reviews on Google and Trustpilot. Warn other authors.
  • Hire a reputable company to fix what you can. Self Publishing Australia offers manuscript rescue and cover redesigns.

Do not feel embarrassed. Scammers are sophisticated. Focus on moving forward.


Frequently asked questions


How do I know if a self publishing company is legitimate?

Check their pricing transparency, client testimonials, and contract terms. Legitimate companies never ask for rights or royalties.

What is the average cost for a reliable self publishing package in Australia?

Most authors spend $4,000 to $8,000 on editing, cover, layout, and basic marketing. Anything far outside that range needs scrutiny.

Can I trust companies that ask for payment upfront?

Yes, most legitimate companies require payment before work. But ask for a detailed contract and a sample first. Never pay the full amount without seeing quality.
